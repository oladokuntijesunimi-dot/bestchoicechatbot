SECTION 1: ABOUT MOLETE (IBADAN) BEST CHOICE COOPERATIVE SOCIETY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Molete (Ibadan) Best Choice Multipurpose Co-operative Society Limited is a registered
multipurpose cooperative society operating under the Co-operative Societies Law of Oyo State.
Founded to promote the social and economic interests of its members, the cooperative is
built on the principles of thrift, mutual support, and cooperative spirit. It serves
as a vehicle to improve living standards, encourage investment, and run profitable ventures
for the collective benefit of its general body.

Our Mission: To encourage thrift and mutual help among members, providing accessible means
to secure savings under member control, grant affordable credit for productive or welfare purposes,
and facilitate consumer goods distribution and property acquisition.

Office Address: First Bank Building, Challenge, Ibadan, Oyo State, Nigeria.
Website: www.bestchoicecooperativesociety.com
Area of Operations: Oyo State and its environs.
Financial Year: 1st May to 31st April.

SECTION 2: MEMBERSHIP REQUIREMENTS & LIABILITY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Eligibility & Qualifications:

Must be a staff member of FBN Holding or any of its subsidiaries.

Must be aged 18 years and above (except in the case of a minor of a deceased member).

Must be of verified good character.

Must not belong to any other society whose primary object is to grant loans to its members,
unless exempted by the registrar.

Every member must sign or thumbprint the membership and attendance register upon admission.

Registration Process:

Application must be submitted in writing through the Secretary.

Review and admission approval are determined by the elected Executive Committee.

If refused, applicants will receive stated reasons and hold the right to appeal to the
next general meeting through an existing member.

Entrance/Admission Fee: ₦5,000 (Reviewable).

Non-refundable administrative exit charge: ₦5,000 (Reviewable in the event of leaving).

Membership Obligations & Liability:

Financial Declaration: Members must render a true, complete statement of any outside
indebtedness to the management committee upon joining.

Nominee Designation: Every member must nominate a beneficiary in writing for their shares/sums
in the event of death or permanent insanity. Nominees are recorded in the register and
payouts require official documentation.

Member Liability: Limited strictly to the nominal value of one held share.
Past members remain liable for existing debts for up to two years after leaving.

Termination of Membership:

Membership terminates upon death, permanent insanity, expulsion, failure to hold one full share,
or written withdrawal.

Voluntary withdrawal requires 3 months' written notice and a minimum membership duration of
6 months (excepting staff laid off by FBN Holdings).

Expulsion: Occurs by simple majority vote at a general meeting for criminal convictions,
prejudicial misconduct, or missing consecutive thrift savings/debt payments for 3 months.

SECTION 3: FUNDS & SAVINGS PLANS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 Share Capital & Minimum Holding

Each share is valued at ₦50 and must be fully paid upon allotment.

Minimum holding requirement: 200 units of shares per member.

Maximum holding limit: No single member can hold more than 20% of the total subscribed share capital.

Shares are not unconditionally withdrawable unless a member completely exits the society or establishment.

3.2 Thrift / Regular Monthly Savings

Managed through continuous funding from members via monthly dues, share installments, and special deposits.

Developmental Levy: A mandatory monthly fee of ₦100 (or as advised by executives) is collected.

Withdrawing Savings: Savings balances cannot be used to liquidate active loans unless the member is
terminating or receives exceptional executive approval due to severe financial distress.

3.3 Fixed Deposits & External Borrowing

The society accepts and accounts for member fixed deposits under regulatory rules.

Liquid funds are held in secure bank accounts operated by the designated Trustees.

External borrowing or external deposit collection requires explicit general assembly approval.

Office cash-on-hand is strictly limited to a maximum of ₦20,000 for immediate working capital.

SECTION 4: LOAN PRODUCTS & SPECIAL facilities
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4.1 Personal Loan

Purpose: General welfare or productive use.

Eligibility: Minimum 6 months of active membership with consecutive, un-defaulted monthly contributions.

Maximum Amount: 2× (double) the member's total savings balance.

Interest Rates: 10% flat interest rate for a 12-month tenure | 15% flat interest rate for an 18-month tenure.

Guarantors: 2 active cooperative members must guarantee half of the loan amount. No guarantors are
required if the total loan request falls strictly within the member's personal savings.

Application Fee: ₦1,000 paid to account 3059522679.

Restrictions: Cannot be run concurrently with an emergency loan or another personal loan. Top-ups
are permitted by deducting the existing balance from a fresh application. Ex-staff who remain members
can only borrow up to 100% of their savings.

4.2 Emergency Loan

Eligibility: Minimum 6 months of active membership with clean history. Executives may exceptionally
approve fractions of savings for members under 6 months old.

Maximum Amount: Limited strictly to the total amount the member has saved with the cooperative.

Interest Rate: 5% flat interest rate for a maximum duration of 6 months.

Repayment: Flexible monthly installments or full payment at the expiration of the 6-month tenor.

Guarantors: Not required.

Application Fee: ₦1,000 paid to account 3059522679.

Restrictions: Blocks access to new personal loans until fully cleared. Top-ups and conversions
to personal loans are available via executive guidance.

4.3 Telephone Loan

Maximum Amount: ₦150,000 (Subject to change via official communication).

Interest Rate: 10% flat interest rate per annum.

Repayment Period: Maximum tenure of 12 months.

Eligibility: 6 months active membership with no contribution defaults in the preceding 6 months.

Guarantors: Not required.

Application Fee: ₦1,000 paid to account 3059522679.

Special Advantage: Can be applied for concurrently while running an active personal or emergency loan.

4.4 Household Loan

Availability: Facilities are issued exclusively in December of each year, running from January to December.

Maximum Amount: Determined annually by the Executive Committee.

Interest Rate: 10% flat interest rate per annum running for up to 12 months.

Eligibility: 6 months of active membership with consecutive, clean contribution records.

Guarantors: Not required.

Application Fee: Free / None required.

Restrictions: Can run alongside personal or emergency loans, but cannot be paired with an active Telephone loan.

4.5 Commodity & Seasonal Loans (Christmas, Ileya, and Itunu-Awe)

Commodity Loan: Short-cycle facility running quarterly (e.g., May–July, Aug–Oct) for a 3-month period at a 3% flat
interest rate. Members can request direct cash options instead of merchandise procurement.

Seasonal Loans: Short-cycle facilities for major celebrations, carrying a 2-month repayment period at a 5% flat
interest rate.

Maximum Amounts: Dynamic; ceilings and special terms are communicated by the executives prior to launch periods.

Eligibility: Granted explicitly at the discretion of the executive team based on the applicant's credit history.

SECTION 5: LOAN MANAGEMENT & CREDIT CONDITIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The 60% Debt Rule: If a member's cumulative monthly debt payments (across all active loans) exceed 60% of their
verifiable monthly salary, the Loan Committee reserves the right to reduce the loan approval amount.

Loan Disbursal to Registered Societies: Loans may be extended to external registered societies provided it benefits
the cooperative, holds executive and loan committee consent, and is ratified by a member majority.

Order of Debt Appropriation: Payments received are applied systematically: first to clearing outstanding fees/fines,
secondly to loan interest, and finally to the loan principal balance.

Immediate Recall Rights: The society reserves the right to demand immediate, full repayment of any active loan if
misapplication is proven, or if the borrowing member hints at resignation or corporate exit.

SECTION 6: PENALTIES, DEFAULTS & ASSET FREERZING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Interest Penalties: Unpaid balances on defaulted loans face a strict monthly penal charge of 2.5%.

Salary Account Freeze: If a borrower defaults on a repayment instance, a direct debit freeze will be initiated
against their primary First Bank staff account until the outstanding liability is cleared.

Forced Contribution Liquidation: If the society is forced to offset defaulted personal loans using a member's
saved contributions, that member will forfeit all borrowing privileges for 1 full year. For emergency and
telephone loans, forced liquidation results in a 6-month borrowing ban.

Post-Default Borrowing Caps: Any member who defaults more than once on their obligations will see their future
borrowing capacity permanently capped at 100% of their personal savings balance.

Litigation: Irrecoverable funds or losses owed to the society will be aggressively pursued through formal litigation.

Meeting Fines: The General Meeting may levy fines up to ₦1,000 for meeting lateness, unexcused absence, or disorder.

SECTION 7: WELFARE, SURPLUS DISPOSAL & AGM GIFTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Surplus & Dividend Allocations:

Year-end net surpluses are calculated by deducting overhead and audit fees from income.

10% is automatically diverted into an indivisible Reserve Fund; 5% to Education Funds; and 5% as
honorariums for executives.

Dividends are paid on member contributions based on annual operational results, subject to executive approval.

Defaulter Dividend Clawback: Dividends belonging to any defaulting member will be automatically seized and redirected
to pay off their outstanding cooperative exposure.

Welfare & Nominee Rights:

In the event of a member’s death or permanent insanity, accumulated shares and savings balances—minus any debts owed
to the society—are officially paid out to their legally designated nominee. Letters of administration
may be requested by the President if familial estate disagreements surface.

AGM Souvenir & Gift Qualifications:

Clean Record Requirement: Members must have zero defaults across contributions, savings, share investments,
development levies, and repayments during the financial year. Defaulting 2 or more times results in total forfeiture
of AGM gifts.

Tenure Requirement: Members must be at least 6 months old and have made 6 full contributions by April 30th of the
reporting year to qualify.

Account Actions: Members who terminated and returned within the same financial year, or members who liquidated
any portion of an active loan using their savings contributions, completely forfeit rights to AGM gifts.

SECTION 8: GOVERNANCE & SPECIALIST COMMITTEES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The Executive Committee: Comprises at least 7 qualified members over the age of 21, including the President,
Vice-Presidents, Treasurer, and Secretary. Elected annually for up to 4 consecutive terms.
Meets at least once a month, requiring a 4-member quorum.

The Trustees: Consists of the President, Vice President, Secretary, and a selected general member. They hold
legal authority to sign cheques, manage properties, and authorize bank activities on behalf of the cooperative.

Advisory Committee (Elders Forum): A 4-member panel (composed of a Chairman, men, and women) holding at least 10 years
of professional working experience. Appointed annually by executives for up to 4 years to handle guidance
and dispute resolution.

Loan Committee: A 3-member specialized panel appointed annually by the executives (max 4-year tenure) tasked with
scrutinizing and recommending approval paths for all loans exceeding ₦50,000.

Audit Committee: Comprises at least 2 members appointed annually by the executives (max 4-year tenure) to review and
internally audit all financial books of accounts alongside ministry audits.

Electoral Committee: Appointed jointly by the Executive and Advisory committees to screen candidates and run transparent
cooperative elections in partnership with the state registrar.

Board of Enquiry: Formed using an odd number of members selected by the Executive and Advisory committees to resolve
internal disputes. Recommendations are submitted for executive ratification; majority consensus across both
ruling bodies breaks deadlocks.
